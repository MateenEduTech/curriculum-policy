📌 Conceptual Background of the App

The NCF 2023 Official Framework Explorer is a fully offline Progressive Web App (PWA) designed to make the National Curriculum Framework 2023 — India's most transformative curriculum document since independence — accessible to every teacher, DIET faculty member, academic leader, and school administrator, even without internet connectivity.

The National Curriculum Framework 2023 (NCF 2023) is the operationalisation of the National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020). NEP 2020 envisions a holistic, multidisciplinary, flexible, learner-centred, competency-based education system that nurtures critical thinking, creativity, communication, and collaboration. NCF 2023 translates these aspirations into concrete curricular structures, pedagogical approaches, learning standards, and assessment strategies for all four stages of school education.

This app preserves the official, original wording of NCF 2023 — unparaphrased, uninterpreted — so that educators always engage with the authentic source document rather than secondary interpretations.

🎯 What Problems Does This App Solve?

  • Accessibility Gap: NCF 2023 is a lengthy, complex document. This app organises it into clearly navigable stages and themes, making it accessible to all educators regardless of digital literacy level.
  • Connectivity Gap: In remote areas of J&K and across India, internet connectivity is unreliable. This offline PWA works perfectly without any internet connection once installed.
  • Authenticity Gap: Secondary summaries and interpretations of NCF 2023 often distort its intent. This app presents only official, original wording.
  • Training Gap: DIET faculty, Cluster Resource Coordinators, and school leaders need reliable reference material for NEP 2020 and NCF 2023 training sessions. This app serves as a portable training companion.
  • Implementation Gap: Teachers need to connect daily classroom practice to the NCF framework. The stage-wise, theme-wise organisation enables direct curricular mapping.

🇮🇳 NEP 2020 & NCF 2023 Orientation

NEP 2020 establishes a new 5+3+3+4 school structure replacing the old 10+2 model. NCF 2023 implements this through four stages:

  • Foundational Stage (5 years): Pre-school through Grade 2 — play-based, activity-based learning emphasising oral language, numeracy, and early literacy.
  • Preparatory Stage (3 years): Grades 3-5 — gradual transition from play-based to formal learning, with emphasis on discovery and experiential methods.
  • Middle Stage (3 years): Grades 6-8 — subject-based curriculum with abstract conceptualisation, introduction of scientific thinking and social sciences.
  • Secondary Stage (4 years): Grades 9-12 — multidisciplinary, flexible curriculum with Board examinations redesigned for competency-based assessment.

NCF 2023 also introduces transformative themes across all stages: Multilingualism, Art Integration, Vocational Integration, Inclusive Education, Environmental Sustainability, and Ethical Citizenship — all of which are covered in this app.

User Manual

How to Use This App

Complete guide to every feature and function

📲 Installation

To install as a Mobile App (Android): Open this app in Chrome browser. Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in Chrome, then tap "Add to Home screen" or "Install app." The app icon will appear on your home screen and function like a native Android app.

To install on Desktop (Windows/Mac): In Chrome or Edge, look for the install icon (➕) in the address bar and click it. Or use the browser menu → "Install NCF 2023."

Offline Use: Once installed, the app works fully offline. All content is cached locally. No internet is needed after first installation.

🧭 Navigation

Menu Button (☰): Opens the sidebar navigation. Tap any section title to navigate directly to it.

Sidebar Filter: Type in the search box at the top of the sidebar to filter navigation items by keyword.

Back Button: The "← Back" button on any content page returns you to the landing home screen.

Home: Tap the "NCF 2023" text in the top header to return to the landing page from anywhere.

Section Cards: On the home screen, tap any section card to open that section directly.

📖 Reading Content

Collapsible Sections: Within each content page, topics are organised into collapsible cards. Tap the header to expand or collapse each topic. This helps you navigate large sections efficiently.

Expand All: Tap the "↕ Expand All" button in the page header to open all sections on the current page at once — useful for printing or reviewing all content.

Reading Progress: The coloured bar at the very top of the screen shows your reading progress as you scroll down any page.

Highlighted Boxes: Saffron-coloured boxes highlight key principles. Blue boxes highlight definitions, standards, or important concepts.

🔍 Search Function

How to Search: Tap the "🔍 Search" button in the top header to open the search modal. Type any keyword (e.g., "competency", "assessment", "multilingualism", "foundational stage").

Search Results: Results show the section name and a snippet of matching text with the keyword highlighted. Tap any result to go directly to that section.

Search Scope: Search covers all section titles and content descriptions — across all 40+ sections of NCF 2023.

Close Search: Tap "✕" or press Escape to close the search modal.

🔖 Bookmarks

Save a Section: On any content page, tap "🔖 Save" in the page header to bookmark that section. The button turns orange to indicate it is saved.

View Bookmarks: Tap "🔖 Saved" in the top header to open the bookmarks panel, showing all your saved sections.

Navigate to Bookmark: Tap any bookmarked item in the panel to go directly to that section.

Remove Bookmark: Tap the "✕" next to any saved item to remove it from bookmarks.

Persistence: Bookmarks are saved in your browser's LocalStorage and persist between sessions, even offline.

🌙 Dark Mode

Tap the "🌙" button in the top header to switch between Light and Dark mode. Dark mode is easier on the eyes in low-light environments. Your preference is automatically saved and restored on the next visit.

🖨️ Print

Tap "🖨️ Print" on any content page to open the browser print dialog. The app is designed with a print-friendly layout — navigation and interactive elements are hidden in print output, leaving only the clean content text suitable for physical handouts or PDF export.

Tip for DIET Sessions: Expand all sections first (using "↕ Expand All"), then print to get the complete section content.

📚 Stage-wise Sections for Training

For Foundational Stage Training (Pre-Primary & Grades 1-2): Focus on Sections 5 (Foundational Stage), 11 (Experiential Learning), 13 (Multilingualism), and 16 (Art-Integrated Learning). These sections directly address the play-based, activity-based methodology central to the Foundational Stage.

For Preparatory Stage Training (Grades 3-5): Focus on Sections 6 (Preparatory Stage), 10 (Pedagogical Approaches), 12 (Competency-Based Education), and 17 (Vocational Integration).

For Middle Stage Training (Grades 6-8): Focus on Sections 7 (Middle Stage), 14 (Assessment Reform), 22 (Learning Standards), and 23 (Cross-Curricular Themes).

For Secondary Stage Training (Grades 9-12): Focus on Sections 8 (Secondary Stage), 14 (Assessment Reform), 20 (School Culture), and 21 (Academic Planning).

For School Leadership Training: Focus on Sections 19 (Teacher Role Transformation), 20 (School Culture), 21 (Academic Planning), and 9 (Curriculum Design Principles).
